Your Stay

On Arrival

  1. Obtain key from keypad situated under a black plastic cover on wall on the right of the front door. Type in the four digit code and open the box. You may need a torch if arriving in dark. To replace front of box you need to type in the four digit code again. If there are any problems opening the box there is another spare key “hidden” in the complex – just give me a ring for whereabouts!

  2. Make a note of your arrival meter reading for Flat 2 in communal meter shed round the back of the apartments – power and heating switches should already be on. A wooden door with a key in the lock is the entrance to the meter shed in the "cloisters" round the back next to garage doors.

  3. Turn on the mains water supply to Bridge Cottage with the stop-tap lever near toilet in downstairs bathroom.

  4. Water and heat switches are in the downstairs bathroom cupboard. Immersion heater and central heating are independent of each other. For hot water turn on socket one and press switch down to I on socket two. It is unnecessary to leave hot water on continuously. A couple of hours in the morning and evening should be adequate.

  5. For central heating there are three controls on socket three. Up is "timer", middle is “off” and “down” is continuous which overrides everything else. Make sure heating insulation switch is on which is above controls. There is a thermostat control on lounge wall near dining table. You will need to turn it up to probably 20-25 degrees for your stay.

On Departure


  1. Check central heating socket three is in "off” position. However in cold winter months, when notified by the owner, please leave central heating socket three in "continuous" position. Also in the winter turn thermostat in lounge down to black mark just below 10 degrees. This is a rule of the complex to avoid bursts.

  2. Check socket two water is on 0 and not 1. Turn off socket one.

  3. Turn off water lever stop tap near toilet.

  4. Check final electric reading, leaving power and heating switches for Flat 2 on.

  5. Please ensure cottage is left clean and tidy for next visitors with no perishable food left in the bread bin, cupboards or fridge. The vacuum cleaner is in the landing cupboard.

  6. Replace key in keybox.

What to take?

Pillow cases, sheets, quilt covers (there are two double continental quilts and four single quilts which should be left on the beds). You may prefer to take sleeping bags. Please take soap and towels.

Location

Bridge Cottage, 2 Starnthwaite Ghyll, Crosthwaite, Cumbria, LA8 8JN


Get directions

M6 until Junction 36 signposted South Lakes, Kendal, Barrow. First left off motorway following A590 Barrow signs. After three miles turn left again at roundabout, still following A590 Barrow signs. Another 2.5 miles turn right onto A5074 signposted Bowness, Windermere. Immediately left after Gilpin Bridge Hotel, up A5074. Follow road for 4.8 miles. Ignore first signs for Kendal, Crosthwaite before 4.8 mark. After 4.8 miles turn right signposted Crosthwaite, Kendal. After just less than 0.5 miles turn left on minor road (signposted Starnthwaite/Crook). If you reach the Old Post Office near a phone box on the left you've gone too far! After turning left, ignore small lane on left to Hubbersty Head and follow Starnthwaite/Crook sign. Pass a few white cottages on left after 0.5 miles. Soon after, turn left into Starnthwaite Ghyll. Be warned, you may miss it. It’s easy to drive straight past! Bridge Cottage is first on the right down the hill. It is advisable to park in the main car-park round the back (near communal dustbins).
